## **Definitions**

**Regulation (EU) No 1169/2011**  
Regulation (EU) No 1169/2011 is an EU law that sets rules for food labeling. It requires information such as ingredients, allergens, nutrition values, and expiry dates so consumers can understand what is in the food they buy.

**Consolidated Version**  
A consolidated version is a document that combines the original law with all later amendments into a single updated text. This helps readers understand the current rules without having to check multiple separate legal documents.

**EUR-Lex**  
EUR-Lex is the official website where legal documents of the European Union are published. It allows users to search, read, and download EU laws and is considered the main source for EU legislation.

**FSANZ**  
Food Standards Australia New Zealand is the agency responsible for developing food standards for Australia and New Zealand. It sets rules for food safety, ingredients, and labeling to protect consumers and maintain food quality.

**Australia New Zealand Food Standards Code**  
The Australia New Zealand Food Standards Code is a set of regulations that control the safety, composition, and labeling of food sold in Australia and New Zealand.

**Standard 1.2.3**  
Standard 1.2.3 requires certain ingredients, especially allergens, to be clearly declared on food labels so consumers can easily identify substances that may cause allergies or intolerances.

**Mandatory Declaration**  
Mandatory declaration means that specific ingredients or substances must be listed on a food label. This requirement helps ensure consumers are informed about important ingredients, especially allergens that could affect their health.

**Quantity Threshold**  
Quantity threshold refers to the minimum amount of a substance that must be present in a food product before it needs to be declared on the label. These limits help determine when labeling becomes necessary.

**Quantity-Independent**  
Quantity-independent means that a substance must be declared on a food label regardless of the amount present. Even very small quantities must be listed to ensure consumers are properly informed.

**Precautionary Allergen Labelling**  
Precautionary allergen labelling includes voluntary warnings such as “may contain nuts” on food packaging. These statements alert consumers to possible allergen contamination during manufacturing or processing.

**Prescribed Label Format**  
Prescribed label format refers to the required way food information must appear on packaging. This may include rules about font size, placement on the label, and how information like ingredients and allergens is displayed.

**Primary source**  
A Primary source is an original and authoritative document that provides direct information about a law, regulation, or event. In legal research, primary sources include official legislation, regulations, treaties, and court decisions published by recognized authorities.

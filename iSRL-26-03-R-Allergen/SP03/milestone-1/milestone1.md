## Concepts I Needed to Understand (GLOSSARY)

1. allergen
	- a substance that can cause an allergic reaction
2. allergen declaration
	- it is when a substance or ingredient is listed on some items where they were used in making or are present
3. jurisdiction
	- in context of food, jurisdiction is the power given to certain govt agencies, officials, or courts to regulate and enforce food saftey laws and standards within a certain area or over some food items 
4. precautionary allergen labelling (PAL)
	- voluntarily labelling to indicate that one or more regulated allergens could be unintentionally, but unavoidably be present in a product that might be at risk to certain people 
5. quantity threshold 
	- the minimum quantity of some hazarodus substance thats stored or processed at an industry that can pose a defined level of risk to those who are susceptible 
6. primary source
	- the original, first-hand account or source of something 
7. food labelling regulation
	- (under UK regulations) food labels should give you information about the food inside the packaging, shelf life and storage instructions to help you make informed decisions about the foods you buy
8. FALCPA
	- (under US regulations) Food Allergen Labeling and Consumer Protection Act of 2004 (FALCPA) 
9. food labelling act (japan)
	- purpose of this act is in light of the important role that food labeling plays in ensuring the safety of food for ingestion and aiding the public
10. cross contamination 
	- the process by which bacteria or other microorganisms are unintentionally transferred from one substance or object to another, with harmful effect
11. ingredients list 
	- a mandatory, detailed rundown of every component used to make a food product, usually listed in descending order by weight (but depends, and also, it includes additives, preservatives, and coloring)

## Dead Ends (EU)

**Explicit Quantity Threshold**

The regulation was examined for provisions establishing a numerical threshold for allergen declaration, such as a minimum level expressed in milligrams per kilogram. No such general threshold was identified in Article 9, Article 21, or Annex II. Article 9(1)(c) simply requires declaration of allergens that remain "present in the finished product." The absence of any qualifying language regarding quantity indicates that EU law adopts a presence-based approach to allergen declaration. The only quantitative reference found was in Annex II, item 12, which requires declaration of sulphites at concentrations exceeding 10 mg/kg or 10 mg/l, but this is a specific requirement for a particular substance rather than a general threshold applicable to all allergens.

**Precautionary Allergen Labelling**

The regulation was also reviewed for provisions governing precautionary allergen statements such as "may contain nuts" or "may contain traces of peanuts." No article within Regulation (EU) No 1169/2011 directly regulates these statements. Precautionary allergen labelling therefore appears to operate on a voluntary basis in the EU, with guidance potentially provided through other documents rather than binding provisions in this regulation.
